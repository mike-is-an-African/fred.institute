C2·Sentence Structure
Parts of a Sentence
Every sentence has two main parts.
SUBJECTSUBJECT: who or what the sentence is about PREDICATE: what the subject does or is
"The tall girl | kicked the ball."
Subject: The tall girl
Predicate: kicked the ball
Simple subject = main nounSimple subject = main noun: girl Simple predicate = main verb: kicked
C2·Sentence Structure
Finding the Subject
Ask WHO or WHAT before the verb.
MethodMethod: Find the verb first, then ask "Who?" or "What?" does this.
"The old man walked slowly." → Who walked? → The old man = subject
"Running is good exercise." → What is good exercise? → Running = subject
"Behind the door stood a cat." → What stood? → a cat = subject (inverted order)

Complete vs Simple
Understanding sentence components.
Complete subject = all words describing the subject
Simple subject = the main noun only
"The very tall boy in the red shirt" → simple subject"The very tall boy in the red shirt" → simple subject: boy "ate his lunch quickly" → simple predicate: ate
