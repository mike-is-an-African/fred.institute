C5 · Area
What is Area?
The amount of space inside a 2D shape.

Area measures the surface covered by a shape. It is always measured in square units — cm², m², km², etc.

Area Formulas Square: A = s²
Rectangle: A = l × w
Triangle: A = ½ × base × height
Parallelogram: A = base × height (perpendicular!)
Trapezium: A = ½(a + b) × h
Circle: A = πr²
Height in area formulas ALWAYS means perpendicular height — the vertical distance, NOT the slant side!
Area is 2D — measured in square units (cm², m²). Perimeter is 1D — measured in linear units (cm, m).

Triangle & Circle Area
Triangle Area A = ½ × base × perpendicular height

Worked example: base=9cm, height=6cm
A = ½ × 9 × 6 = 27 cm²
Circle Area A = πr²

If r=7cm: A = π×7² = π×49 ≈ 153.9 cm²
If diameter=10cm: r=5, A=π×25≈78.5 cm²
For a triangle: the height must be perpendicular to the base — even if the triangle looks tilted!

Area of Compound Shapes
Method for Compound Shapes Split the shape into simple shapes (rectangles, triangles, circles, etc.)
Calculate each area separately.
Add them together (or subtract if a piece is removed).

Example — L-shape: Split into 2 rectangles.
Rectangle A: 6×4=24cm². Rectangle B: 3×2=6cm².
Total = 24+6 = 30 cm²

Trapezium & Parallelogram Area
Parallelogram A = base × perpendicular height
NOT base × slant side!

Base=8cm, perpendicular height=5cm:
A = 8×5 = 40 cm²
Trapezium A = ½(a + b) × h
where a and b are the parallel sides and h is the perpendicular height

a=5cm, b=9cm, h=4cm:
A = ½(5+9)×4 = ½×14×4 = 28 cm²
