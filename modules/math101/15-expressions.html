Expressions vs Equations
Two very different things — don't mix them up!
Algebraic ExpressionA mathematical phrase with numbers, variables, and operations — but NO equals sign.

Examples: 3x + 5, 2a − b, x², 4(y + 3)
Algebraic EquationContains an equals sign (=). States that two expressions are equal.

Examples: 3x + 5 = 14, 2a − b = 7
TermA single number, variable, or product of numbers & variables.
In 3x + 5 − y²: the terms are 3x, 5, and −y².
Expression = no equals sign. Equation = has an equals sign. This distinction matters everywhere in algebra!

Expanding Brackets
Multiply everything inside the bracket by what's outside.
Single Bracket Expansion Multiply the term OUTSIDE by EVERY term inside.

3(x + 4) = 3×x + 3×4 = 3x + 12
−2(5 − 3y) = −2×5 + (−2)×(−3y) = −10 + 6y
x(x + 7) = x×x + x×7 = x² + 7x
Careful with negative signs! −3(x − 4) = −3x + 12. The two negatives give a positive.
Expand: 4(2x − 3)

Factorising Expressions
The reverse of expanding — take out the common factor.
Factorising (Simple) Find the HCF of all terms. Take it outside the bracket. Whatever's left goes inside.

6x + 15: HCF = 3
= 3(2x + 5)

12x² − 8x: HCF = 4x
= 4x(3x − 2)
Check by expanding: 3(2x+5) = 6x+15 ✓. Always verify your factorisation.

Expanding Double Brackets
FOIL: First, Outer, Inner, Last.
FOIL Method (x + 3)(x + 5):
First: x×x = x²
Outer: x×5 = 5x
Inner: 3×x = 3x
Last: 3×5 = 15
= x² + 5x + 3x + 15 = x² + 8x + 15
Expand (x + 2)(x + 6). What is the constant term?

Key Points
  • Expression: no equals sign. Equation: has an equals sign.
  • Expanding: multiply outside term by EVERY term inside bracket
  • Factorising: take out HCF, write remaining terms in bracket
  • FOIL for double brackets: First, Outer, Inner, Last — then collect like terms
  • Negative × negative = positive (watch signs!)
  • Always check by expanding your factorisation
