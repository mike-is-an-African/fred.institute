What is a Ratio?
Comparing two or more quantities of the same kind.

A ratio compares quantities of the same type. If a class has 12 boys and 18 girls, the ratio of boys to girls is 12:18, simplified to 2:3.

RatioWritten as a:b or a/b. Shows the relative size of two or more values.
12 boys : 18 girls → 12:18 → 2:3 (divide both by HCF=6)
Simplifying RatiosDivide all parts by their HCF — same as simplifying fractions.
15:25 → HCF=5 → 3:5
8:12:20 → HCF=4 → 2:3:5
Equivalent RatiosMultiply or divide all parts by the same number.
2:3 = 4:6 = 6:9 = 20:30
Ratio has NO units — it compares like quantities. 3 km : 5 km = 3:5 (drop "km").

Sharing Amounts in a Given Ratio
Steps to Share in a Ratio 1. Add all parts of the ratio to get total parts
2. Divide the whole amount by total parts → 1 part value
3. Multiply each ratio number by the 1-part value

Example: Share $60 in ratio 2:3:
Total parts = 2+3 = 5
1 part = 60÷5 = $12
Share A: 2×12 = $24
Share B: 3×12 = $36
Check: 24+36 = 60 ✓
Total amount $:60
Part A:2
Part B:3
A=$24 · B=$36

Rate
Comparing two quantities of DIFFERENT types.
Rate vs Ratio Ratio: same units (boys:girls). No units shown.
Rate: DIFFERENT units. The units ARE included.

Examples of rates:
Speed: 60 km per hour (km/h)
Price: $3.50 per kg ($/kg)
Heartbeat: 72 beats per minute (bpm)
Wage: $18 per hour
Unit RateA rate with 1 as the second quantity. Easiest to compare.
$4.50 for 3 kg → $4.50÷3 = $1.50/kg (unit rate)
A car uses 45 litres of fuel to travel 630 km. What is the fuel efficiency in km per litre?

Scale Drawings & Maps
ScaleA ratio that tells you how measurements on a drawing relate to real measurements.

Scale 1:50 means 1 cm on the drawing = 50 cm in real life.
Scale 1:100,000 means 1 cm on map = 1 km in real life.
Example: A map has scale 1:25,000. Two towns are 8 cm apart on the map. What is the real distance?
Real distance = 8 × 25,000 = 200,000 cm = 2 km
A model car uses scale 1:20. The real car is 4.4 m long. How long is the model in cm?

Key Points
  • Ratio: compares same-unit quantities (a:b). Simplify by ÷ HCF.
  • Sharing: find total parts, divide amount, multiply each share
  • Rate: compares different-unit quantities (km/h, $/kg)
  • Unit rate: the rate when second quantity = 1
  • Scale: ratio of drawing measurement to real measurement
  • Real = map × scale. Map = real ÷ scale
