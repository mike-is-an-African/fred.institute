Forming Equations from Words
Translate a problem into algebra, then solve.
Steps to Form an Equation 1. Identify the unknown — call it x (or another letter)
2. Write expressions for other quantities in terms of x
3. Find an equality condition (what equals what?)
4. Write the equation and solve
5. Check the answer makes sense
Example: "A number doubled then increased by 5 gives 17. Find the number."
Let number = x
Doubled: 2x. Increased by 5: 2x + 5
Equation: 2x + 5 = 17
2x = 12 → x = 6

Introduction to Inequalities
Like equations, but with >, <, ≥, ≤ instead of =.
Inequality Symbols > means "greater than"
< means "less than"
≥ means "greater than or equal to"
≤ means "less than or equal to"
Solving Inequalities Same as equations EXCEPT: when you multiply or divide by a NEGATIVE number, FLIP the inequality sign.

Solve 2x + 3 < 11:
2x < 8 → x < 4

Solve −3x ≥ 9:
÷(−3) → x ≤ −3 (flip!)
Solve: 5x − 2 > 13. What is x?

Simultaneous Equations (Intro)
Two equations, two unknowns — solve together.
Substitution Method Rearrange one equation to get y = … then substitute into the other.

x + y = 10 and x − y = 4
Add equations: 2x = 14 → x = 7
Substitute: 7 + y = 10 → y = 3
From: x + y = 8 and x − y = 2. What is x?
