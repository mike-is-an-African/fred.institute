C5 · 2D Shapes
2D Shape Vocabulary
Every term you need to know.

2D (two-dimensional) shapes are flat — they have length and width but no depth. Every polygon has sides, vertices, and angles.

Key Terms Polygon: a closed 2D shape with straight sides
Vertex (plural: vertices): a corner where two sides meet
Side/Edge: a straight line forming part of the boundary
Interior angle: angle INSIDE the shape
Exterior angle: angle OUTSIDE (= 180° − interior angle)
Regular polygon: all sides equal AND all angles equal
Interior Angle Sum Formula For a polygon with n sides:
Sum of interior angles = (n − 2) × 180°

Triangle (n=3): 1×180 = 180°
Quadrilateral (n=4): 2×180 = 360°
Pentagon (n=5): 3×180 = 540°
Hexagon (n=6): 4×180 = 720°

Types of Triangles
Classified by sides AND by angles.
By Sides Equilateral: 3 equal sides, 3 equal angles (60° each)
Isosceles: 2 equal sides, 2 equal base angles
Scalene: all sides different, all angles different
By Angles Acute: all angles < 90°
Right-angled: one angle = exactly 90° (has a square corner mark)
Obtuse: one angle > 90°
Every triangle has exactly 180° total. If you know two angles, subtract from 180° to find the third.

Key Angle Properties
1
Angles on a straight line = 180°
If a + b + c are on one side of a line: a + b + c = 180°
2
Angles around a point = 360°
All angles meeting at one point sum to 360°
3
Vertically opposite angles are equal
When two straight lines cross, opposite angles are identical
4
Exterior angle of a triangle = sum of other two interior angles
The exterior angle equals the sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles

Key Points
  • Interior angle sum: (n−2)×180°
  • Exterior angles of any polygon always sum to 360°
  • Triangle types: equilateral, isosceles, scalene (by side); acute, right, obtuse (by angle)
  • Key formulas: square A=s², rectangle A=lw, triangle A=½bh, circle A=πr²
  • Vertically opposite angles are equal; angles on a line = 180°
