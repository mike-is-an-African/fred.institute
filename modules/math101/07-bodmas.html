What is BODMAS?
A rule that tells you what to calculate first in any expression.

Without rules, 2 + 3 × 4 could equal 20 (if you add first) or 14 (if you multiply first). BODMAS is the universal rule that ensures everyone gets the same answer.

BBrackets ( ) first — always! OOrders — powers and square roots (e.g. 3², √25) DDivision — left to right MMultiplication — left to right (same priority as D) AAddition — left to right SSubtraction — left to right (same priority as A)
Division and Multiplication have EQUAL priority — work left to right. Same for Addition and Subtraction.

Watch the BODMAS rule in action.

Multiplication first: 4×5=20. Then: 3+20 = 23 (NOT 35)
2

Brackets first: 3+4=7. Then: 7×5 = 35
3
2 + 3² × 4
Orders first: 3²=9. Then multiply: 9×4=36. Then add: 2+36 = 38
4
12 ÷ 3 × 2
Division and multiplication — left to right: 12÷3=4, then 4×2 = 8
5
(6 + 2)² ÷ 4 − 3
Brackets: 6+2=8. Orders: 8²=64. Division: 64÷4=16. Subtract: 16−3 = 13

Common BODMAS Mistakes
These trip up almost every student. Learn them now.
Mistake 1: Left-to-right for all operations
8 − 3 + 2 ≠ 3. Correct: 8−3=5, then 5+2=7. ✓
Mistake 2: Forgetting orders (powers)
2 + 3² ≠ 5² = 25. Correct: 3²=9, then 2+9=11. ✓
Mistake 3: Multiplying before division when division is first (left)
12 ÷ 4 × 3 ≠ 1. Correct: 12÷4=3, then 3×3=9. ✓
Mistake 4: Ignoring brackets inside brackets
Work innermost brackets first: ((2+3)×4) = (5×4) = 20. ✓

Nested Brackets & Complex Expressions
Nested BracketsWhen brackets appear inside other brackets, work from the INNERMOST outward.

Types used in maths: ( ) round, [ ] square, { } curly — always innermost first.
Example: 2 × [3 + (4 − 1)²]
Step 1 — innermost round brackets: (4−1) = 3
Step 2 — orders: 3² = 9
Step 3 — addition inside square brackets: 3+9 = 12
Step 4 — multiplication: 2×12 = 24
Calculate: 4 × [(2 + 3) − 1]

Key Points
  • B — Brackets first, innermost outward
  • O — Orders: powers (n²) and roots (√)
  • DM — Division and Multiplication: equal priority, left to right
  • AS — Addition and Subtraction: equal priority, left to right
  • Without BODMAS, the same expression gives different answers
  • Nested brackets: ( ) then [ ] then { } — always innermost first
