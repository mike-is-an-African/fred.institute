What Is a Fraction?
Parts of a whole — the foundation of all fraction work.

A fraction describes part of a whole that has been split into equal parts. A pizza cut into 8 equal slices where you take 3 = you have 3/8 of the pizza.

NumeratorThe TOP number — how many equal parts you HAVE. In ³⁄₈, numerator = 3.
DenominatorThe BOTTOM number — how many equal parts the whole is split INTO. In ³⁄₈, denominator = 8.
The Fraction BarThe line between them means "out of" or "divided by". ³⁄₈ = 3 ÷ 8 = 0.375
The denominator can NEVER be zero — you cannot split something into zero parts. It's undefined!

Types of Fractions
Four types — you must know them all.
Proper FractionNumerator < Denominator. Value is less than 1.
Examples: ¹⁄₂, ³⁄₄, ⁵⁄₈, ²⁄₇
Improper FractionNumerator ≥ Denominator. Value is 1 or greater.
Examples: ⁵⁄₄, ⁹⁄₃, ⁷⁄₂
Mixed NumberA whole number combined with a proper fraction.
Examples: 1¾, 2½, 3⅓
Unit FractionNumerator = 1. These are the simplest fractions.
Examples: ½, ⅓, ¼, ⅕, ⅙
Improper fractions and mixed numbers represent the SAME value. ⁵⁄₄ = 1¼

Converting Between Types
Improper ↔ Mixed number — two key skills.
Improper → Mixed Number Step 1: Divide numerator ÷ denominator
Step 2: Quotient = whole number
Step 3: Remainder = new numerator (keep same denominator)

Example: ¹³⁄₄
13 ÷ 4 = 3 remainder 1
Answer: 3¼
Mixed Number → Improper Step 1: Whole number × Denominator
Step 2: Add the numerator
Step 3: Keep the same denominator

Example: 2⅗
(2 × 5) + 3 = 13
Answer: ¹³⁄₅

Simplifying Fractions
Always reduce to simplest form using the HCF.
Simplifying (Reducing) Divide both numerator AND denominator by their Highest Common Factor (HCF).

Example: Simplify ¹²⁄₁₆
HCF(12, 16) = 4
12 ÷ 4 = 3, 16 ÷ 4 = 4
Answer: ¾
A fraction is in its SIMPLEST form when numerator and denominator share no common factor other than 1.

Key Points
  • Numerator = parts you have (top). Denominator = total equal parts (bottom).
  • Proper: num < den (value <1). Improper: num ≥ den (value ≥1).
  • Mixed number = whole + proper fraction. 2¾ = ¹¹⁄₄
  • Unit fraction: numerator = 1 (½, ⅓, ¼…)
  • Simplify: divide both by HCF until no common factors remain
  • Denominator can NEVER be zero
