What is an Equation?
A statement that two expressions are equal.

An equation uses an equals sign (=) to state that two things have the same value. Solving an equation means finding what value of the unknown makes both sides equal.

Parts of an Equation Left-hand side (LHS) = Right-hand side (RHS)
In 3x + 5 = 14:
LHS = 3x + 5
RHS = 14
Solution: x = 3 (because 3×3+5 = 14 ✓)
The Balance Method An equation is like a balanced scale. Whatever you do to one side, you MUST do the same to the other side to keep the balance.
The solution is the value that makes LHS = RHS. Always check by substituting back.

One-Step Equations
One operation — one step to solve.
Using Inverse Operations To isolate x, do the opposite operation:
+ becomes −. − becomes +. × becomes ÷. ÷ becomes ×.

x + 9 = 14 → x = 14 − 9 = 5
x − 6 = 3 → x = 3 + 6 = 9
4x = 28 → x = 28 ÷ 4 = 7
x/5 = 3 → x = 3 × 5 = 15
Solve: x − 11 = 7
Solve: 8x = 56

Two-Step Equations
Undo operations in REVERSE order of BODMAS.
Two-Step Method Step 1: Add or subtract to move the constant
Step 2: Multiply or divide to isolate x

Solve 3x + 7 = 19:
Step 1: −7 both sides → 3x = 12
Step 2: ÷3 both sides → x = 4

Solve 5x − 3 = 22:
Step 1: +3 → 5x = 25
Step 2: ÷5 → x = 5
Solve: 4x + 5 = 21
Solve: x/3 − 2 = 5

Variables on Both Sides
Collecting Variables to One Side Move all x terms to one side and constants to the other.

Solve 5x + 3 = 2x + 15:
−2x both sides: 3x + 3 = 15
−3 both sides: 3x = 12
÷3: x = 4
Solve: 7x − 4 = 3x + 8

Key Points
  • Equation = LHS = RHS. Goal: find value of unknown.
  • Balance method: same operation on BOTH sides
  • Inverse operations: +↔−, ×↔÷
  • Two-step: move constant first (add/subtract), then coefficient (×÷)
  • Variables both sides: collect all x terms on one side
  • Always check: substitute solution back to verify LHS=RHS
