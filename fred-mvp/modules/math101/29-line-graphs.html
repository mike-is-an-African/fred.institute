What is a Line Graph?

A line graph plots data points and joins them with straight lines. They are ideal for showing how a quantity changes over time — trends, rises, falls, and fluctuations.

Key Features Title: what is being measured
X-axis: usually time (months, years, etc.)
Y-axis: the quantity being measured
Points plotted and connected by straight lines
Can show multiple lines (with a key/legend)
Trends Upward trend: values generally increasing over time
Downward trend: values generally decreasing
No trend: values fluctuating with no clear direction
Monthly temperature (°C):
Jan:8
Apr:15
Jul:28
Oct:18

Reading Line Graphs
1
Find a value at a specific time
Go to that point on the x-axis → trace up to the line → trace across to the y-axis.
2
Find when a value occurred
Go to that value on the y-axis → trace across to the line → trace down to the x-axis.
3
Describe the trend
Is the overall direction up, down, or neither? Mention any unusual peaks or troughs.
A line graph shows temperatures: Mon=12°, Tue=15°, Wed=9°, Thu=18°, Fri=14°. What day had the biggest increase from the previous day?

Scatter Graphs & Correlation
Scatter GraphPlots two variables to see if there is a relationship (correlation) between them. Each point represents one item of data.
Types of Correlation Positive: as one increases, so does the other (e.g. height and weight)
Negative: as one increases, the other decreases (e.g. speed and time)
No correlation: no pattern (e.g. shoe size and maths score)
Line of Best FitA straight line drawn through the middle of the points, with roughly equal numbers above and below. Used to estimate/predict values.
As temperature rises, ice cream sales also rise. What correlation is this?

Time Series & Moving Averages
Time SeriesData collected at regular intervals over time. Plotted as a line graph.
Moving AverageSmooths out fluctuations to show the underlying trend.

4-point moving average: average each group of 4 consecutive values.
Data: 3, 5, 7, 4, 6, 8, 5, 9
First MA: (3+5+7+4)÷4 = 4.75
Second MA: (5+7+4+6)÷4 = 5.5
Data: 10, 14, 8, 12. What is the 4-point moving average?

Key Points
  • Line graphs: show change over time. Connect points with straight lines.
  • Trend: upward, downward, or no trend.
  • Scatter graph: two variables. Shows correlation.
  • Positive: both up. Negative: one up, other down. None: no pattern.
  • Line of best fit: middle of points, used to predict values.
  • Moving average: smooths data. Average every n consecutive values.
