Adding & Subtracting: Same Denominator
The easy case — denominator stays the same.
Same Denominator Rule When denominators are identical: add or subtract ONLY the numerators. Keep the denominator the same.

³⁄₈ + ²⁄₈ = ⁵⁄₈
⁷⁄₉ − ²⁄₉ = ⁵⁄₉
Think of it as: "3 eighths + 2 eighths = 5 eighths". Just like 3 apples + 2 apples = 5 apples!

Adding & Subtracting: Different Denominators
Find a common denominator first — then add.
Steps for Different Denominators Step 1: Find the LCM of the two denominators
Step 2: Convert BOTH fractions to that LCM as denominator
Step 3: Add or subtract the numerators
Step 4: Simplify if possible

Example: ⅓ + ¼
LCM(3,4) = 12
⅓ = ⁴⁄₁₂, ¼ = ³⁄₁₂
⁴⁄₁₂ + ³⁄₁₂ = ⁷⁄₁₂

Multiplying Fractions
Top × top. Bottom × bottom. Then simplify.
Multiplying Fractions Rule Multiply numerators together AND denominators together. Then simplify.

²⁄₃ × ³⁄₄ = (2×3)/(3×4) = ⁶⁄₁₂ = ½

Tip: Cross-cancel BEFORE multiplying to keep numbers smaller:
²⁄₃ × ³⁄₄ → cancel 3s: ²⁄₁ × ¹⁄₄ = ²⁄₄ = ½
Multiplying Mixed Numbers Convert to improper fractions FIRST, then multiply.
1½ × 2⅓ = ³⁄₂ × ⁷⁄₃ = ²¹⁄₆ = 3½

Dividing Fractions
KFC: Keep, Flip, Change (then multiply).
Dividing Fractions: KFC Rule Keep the first fraction as it is
Flip the second fraction (take its reciprocal)
Change ÷ to ×
Then multiply normally

Example: ¾ ÷ ⅔
= ¾ × ³⁄₂ (flip ⅔ → ³⁄₂)
= ⁹⁄₈ = 1⅛
ReciprocalFlip the fraction: swap numerator and denominator.
Reciprocal of ²⁄₅ = ⁵⁄₂
Reciprocal of 4 = ¼
Dividing by a fraction means multiplying by its reciprocal. ÷ ½ is the same as × 2.

Key Points
  • Add/subtract same denom: just add/subtract numerators
  • Add/subtract different denom: find LCM, convert both, then add/subtract
  • Multiply: numerator × numerator, denominator × denominator, then simplify
  • Divide (KFC): Keep first, Flip second, Change to ×
  • Mixed numbers: convert to improper fractions before ×÷
  • Always simplify your final answer
