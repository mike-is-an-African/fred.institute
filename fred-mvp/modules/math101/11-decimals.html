What Are Decimals?
Numbers with a fractional part, written using a point.

Decimals are another way of writing fractions with denominators of 10, 100, 1000, etc. The decimal point separates the whole number part from the fractional part.

Decimal Place Values 3 . 1 4 7
↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑
Units . Tenths Hundredths Thousandths

3 = 3 units
1 = 1 tenth = 0.1 = 1/10
4 = 4 hundredths = 0.04 = 4/100
7 = 7 thousandths = 0.007 = 7/1000
Terminating decimals end: 0.25. Recurring decimals repeat: 0.333... = 0.3̄

Operations with Decimals
Adding, subtracting, multiplying and dividing.
+/−
Adding & Subtracting
Align decimal points. Fill gaps with zeros. Then use column method. 3.4 + 1.27 → 3.40 + 1.27 = 4.67
×
Multiplying
Ignore decimal, multiply as integers, count total decimal places, reinsert. 0.4 × 0.7: 4×7=28, 2dp → 0.28
÷
Dividing
Make divisor a whole number (×10, ×100…). Both numbers multiply. 2.4÷0.6 → ×10 → 24÷6=4

Rounding & Significant Figures
Rounding to Decimal Places (d.p.)Count digits after the decimal. Look at the next digit:
≥5 → round up. <5 → keep.
7.845 to 2dp: look at 3rd dp =5 → round up → 7.85
Significant Figures (s.f.)Count from the FIRST non-zero digit.
0.00347 to 2 s.f.: first sig fig=3, second=4, look at 7 ≥5 → round up → 0.0035
Round 3.8752 to 2 decimal places.

Ordering Decimals
How to Order DecimalsAdd trailing zeros so all decimals have same number of places. Then compare as whole numbers.

Order: 0.3, 0.25, 0.309, 0.31
→ 0.300, 0.250, 0.309, 0.310
→ Ascending: 0.250, 0.300, 0.309, 0.310
= 0.25, 0.3, 0.309, 0.31

Key Points
  • Decimal places: tenths (÷10), hundredths (÷100), thousandths (÷1000)
  • Add/subtract: align decimal points first
  • Multiply: count total dp, multiply as integers, reinsert dp
  • Divide: make divisor a whole number (×10, ×100…)
  • Rounding to dp: look one place further right; ≥5 round up
  • Sig figs: count from first non-zero digit
