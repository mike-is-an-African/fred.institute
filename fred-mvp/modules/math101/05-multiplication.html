Multiplication — Key Vocabulary
The language of multiplication.

Multiplication is repeated addition. 4 × 3 means "four groups of three" = 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12.

FactorThe numbers being multiplied. In 4 × 3 = 12, both 4 and 3 are factors.
ProductThe result of multiplication. In 4 × 3 = 12, the product is 12.
Multiplier & MultiplicandThe multiplicand is the number being multiplied. The multiplier says how many times. In 4 × 3: 4 is the multiplicand, 3 is the multiplier.
Multiplication is commutative: 4 × 3 = 3 × 4. And associative: (2×3)×4 = 2×(3×4).
Sign rules: positive × positive = positive. positive × negative = negative. negative × negative = positive.

Long Multiplication — Step by Step
Multiplying large numbers using the written method.
Long Multiplication Steps 1. Write the larger number on top
2. Multiply top number by the UNITS digit of multiplier
3. Write a zero placeholder, then multiply top by TENS digit
4. Add the two rows together
Example: 247 × 34
     2 4 7
   ×   3 4
   -------
     9 8 8   (247 × 4)
 + 7 4 1 0   (247 × 30, add 0)
 ---------
   8 3 9 8
Answer: 8,398

Multiplying Decimals
Ignore the decimal point, multiply, then put it back.
Steps for Decimal Multiplication 1. Count total decimal places in both numbers
2. Multiply ignoring decimal points (treat as whole numbers)
3. Put decimal point back: count in from right by total decimal places
Example: 3.4 × 2.5
1 decimal place + 1 decimal place = 2 places total
34 × 25 = 850
Move decimal 2 places left: 8.50
Example: 0.6 × 0.7
6 × 7 = 42. Two decimal places → 0.42

Factors & Multiples
Key concepts linked to multiplication.
FactorA number that divides exactly into another with no remainder.
Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12 (all divide 12 exactly)
MultipleThe result of multiplying a number by an integer.
Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24… (4×1, 4×2, 4×3…)
HCF — Highest Common FactorThe largest factor shared by two numbers.
HCF(12, 18): factors of 12={1,2,3,4,6,12}, of 18={1,2,3,6,9,18}. Common={1,2,3,6}. HCF=6.
LCM — Lowest Common MultipleThe smallest number that is a multiple of both.
LCM(4, 6): multiples of 4={4,8,12…}, of 6={6,12…}. LCM=12.

  • Decimals: count total dp, multiply as integers, place dp back
