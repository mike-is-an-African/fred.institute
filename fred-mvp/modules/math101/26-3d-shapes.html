3D Shape Vocabulary
Key Terms Face: a flat surface of a 3D shape
Edge: a line where two faces meet
Vertex: a corner where edges meet (plural: vertices)
Cross-section: the shape you see when you cut through a 3D shape
Euler's Formula For any convex polyhedron:
Faces + Vertices − Edges = 2

Cube: 6+8−12 = 2 ✓
Tetrahedron: 4+4−6 = 2 ✓
Prism vs Pyramid Prism: same cross-section all the way through. Two identical parallel ends.
Pyramid: comes to a point (apex) at the top. One base, triangular faces.

Volume of 3D Shapes
Volume The amount of 3D space inside a shape. Measured in cubic units: cm³, m³.

Cuboid: V = l × w × h
Cube: V = s³
Cylinder: V = πr²h
Cone: V = ⅓πr²h
Sphere: V = ⁴⁄₃πr³
Prism: V = cross-section area × length
Cuboid volume:
l (cm):5
w (cm):4
h (cm):3
V = 60 cm³

Surface Area
Surface Area The total area of ALL the faces of a 3D shape. Measured in cm², m².

To find SA: find the area of EACH face, then add them all.

Cuboid SA = 2(lw + lh + wh)
Cube SA = 6s²
Cylinder SA = 2πr² + 2πrh
Sphere SA = 4πr²

Nets of 3D Shapes
NetA flat shape that can be folded to make a 3D shape. Shows all faces laid flat.

Cube net: 6 squares in a cross or T pattern.
Cuboid net: 6 rectangles in pairs of equal size.
Cylinder net: 2 circles + 1 rectangle (curved surface unrolled).
