Types of Angles
Classification by size.
Acute AngleGreater than 0° and less than 90°. "Sharp" angle. Example: 35°, 72°
Right AngleExactly 90°. Shown with a small square. Perpendicular lines meet at right angles.
Obtuse AngleGreater than 90° and less than 180°. "Blunt" angle. Example: 120°, 155°
Straight AngleExactly 180°. A straight line.
Reflex AngleGreater than 180° and less than 360°. Goes more than halfway around.

Key Angle Rules
These rules appear in every geometry problem.
1
Angles on a straight line = 180°
If angles a, b, c are on one side of a straight line: a+b+c=180°
2
Angles at a point = 360°
All angles meeting at a single point: a+b+c+d=360°
3
Vertically opposite angles are equal
When two lines cross: a=c and b=d (opposite pairs)
4
Angles in a triangle = 180°
Sum of all 3 interior angles in any triangle = 180°
5
Exterior angle = sum of two non-adjacent interior angles
Outside a triangle at a vertex: ext.angle = a + b (the two far angles)
Two angles at a point are 140° and 70°. Find the third angle.

Angles in Parallel Lines
Parallel Lines — Cut by a Transversal When a line (transversal) cuts two parallel lines:

Alternate angles: equal (Z-shape / N-shape)
Corresponding angles: equal (F-shape)
Co-interior angles: add to 180° (C-shape / U-shape)
Memory aid: Alternate angles = Z (zigzag). Corresponding = F (same side). Co-interior = C (add to 180°).
Parallel lines cut by transversal. One angle is 65°. Find the alternate angle.
Find the co-interior angle to 112° in parallel lines.

Bearings
Bearing A three-digit angle measured CLOCKWISE from North.

North = 000°
East = 090°
South = 180°
West = 270°

Always use 3 digits: 45° is written as 045°.
A plane flies South-East. What is the bearing? (South-East is halfway between South and East)

Key Points
  • Acute <90°. Right =90°. Obtuse 90°–180°. Reflex >180°.
  • Straight line=180°. Full turn=360°. Triangle=180°.
  • Vertically opposite: equal. Alternate: equal. Corresponding: equal.
  • Co-interior (same side): add to 180°.
  • Bearings: 3-digit, clockwise from North. N=000°, E=090°, S=180°, W=270°.
