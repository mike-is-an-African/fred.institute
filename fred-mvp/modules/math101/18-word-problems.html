RUCSAC — A Framework for Word Problems
A systematic method that works for every word problem.

Word problems are the most powerful form of maths — they connect equations to real life. Use RUCSAC to never get lost.

RRead the problem carefully (twice!) UUnderline key information & unknowns CChoose the operation(s) needed SSolve the equation or calculation AAnswer the question (with units!) CCheck — does your answer make sense?

Number Word Problems
1
Two numbers add to 50. One is 8 more than the other. Find them.
Let smaller = x. Larger = x+8. x+(x+8)=50. 2x=42. x=21. Numbers: 21 and 29.
2
The product of two consecutive integers is 72. Find them.
Let n and n+1. n(n+1)=72. n²+n−72=0. (n+9)(n−8)=0. n=8. Integers: 8 and 9.
The sum of three consecutive numbers is 48. What is the smallest?

Money & Shopping Problems
Example: 3 pens and 2 notebooks cost $11.50. A pen costs $1.50. How much is a notebook?

Let notebook = $n.
3(1.50) + 2n = 11.50
4.50 + 2n = 11.50
2n = 7.00
n = $3.50

Distance, Speed & Time
The DST Triangle Distance = Speed × Time
Speed = Distance ÷ Time
Time = Distance ÷ Speed

Units must match! km/h with km and hours. m/s with metres and seconds.
A car travels 240 km at 80 km/h. How many hours does the journey take?
A train covers 360 km in 4 hours. What is its average speed in km/h?

Key Points
  • RUCSAC: Read, Underline, Choose, Solve, Answer, Check
  • Always define your unknown: "Let x = …"
  • Include units in your answer
  • DST triangle: D=ST, S=D/T, T=D/S
  • Check: does the answer make sense in real life?
  • Read the question twice before starting
