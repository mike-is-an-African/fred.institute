C1·Parts of Speech
What is a Noun?
A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.

A noun is one of the most important parts of speech. It is any word that names something — a person, a place, a thing, or an idea. Without nouns, we could not talk about the world around us.

Types of NounsCommon nouns: general names — dog, city, book, teacher
Proper nouns: specific names, always capitalised — London, Sarah, Monday
Abstract nouns: ideas or feelings you cannot touch — love, freedom, courage, happiness
Collective nouns: groups — a flock of birds, a team of players, a bunch of grapes
Concrete nouns: things you can see, touch, hear, smell, or taste — table, music, perfume
Every sentence needs at least one noun. The noun is usually the subject — the "doer" of the action.
Identify the nouns: "The girl walked her dog through the park on Sunday."

girl = common noun (person)
dog = common noun (thing)
park = common noun (place)
Sunday = proper noun (specific day)

Singular, Plural & Possessive
Nouns change form depending on number and ownership.
Singular & PluralSingular = one: cat, child, bus
Plural = more than one: cats, children, buses

Regular plurals: add -s or -es (book→books, box→boxes)
Irregular plurals: change the word (child→children, mouse→mice, tooth→teeth, person→people)
Possessive NounsShow ownership using an apostrophe:
Singular possessive: add 's — the dog's bone, James's book
Plural possessive: add ' after the s — the dogs' park, the teachers' room
Irregular plural: add 's — the children's toys
C1·Parts of Speech
Countable & Uncountable Nouns
Some nouns can be counted. Others cannot.
Countable NounsCan be counted: one apple, two apples, three apples
Use a/an with singular: a book, an egg
Use many, few, several with plural: many books, few eggs
Uncountable NounsCannot be counted individually: water, rice, information, advice, furniture
Use much, little, some, a lot of: much water, little advice
NEVER use a/an or add -s: NOT "an information" or "advices"
Some nouns can be both: "I had a coffee" (a cup) vs "I spilled coffee" (the substance).

Noun Functions in a Sentence
Nouns play different roles depending on their position.
Subject: The cat sat on the mat. (Who/what does the action)
Object: She kicked the ball. (Who/what receives the action)
Complement: He is a doctor. (Renames or describes the subject)
Object of preposition: She sat on the chair. (Follows a preposition)
To find the subject noun, ask "Who?" or "What?" before the verb. To find the object noun, ask "Who?" or "What?" after the verb.

Challenge: Noun Identification
Test your skills with longer sentences.
Identify ALL nouns in this sentence:

"The students at FredInstitute completed their assignments with great enthusiasm before the deadline on Friday."

6 nouns: students (common), FredInstitute (proper), assignments (common), enthusiasm (abstract), deadline (common), Friday (proper)
Now try: "The team of scientists made an important discovery about water on Mars."

5 nouns: team (collective), scientists (common), discovery (abstract), water (uncountable), Mars (proper)
